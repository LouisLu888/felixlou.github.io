I abosolutely love this talk, funny, useful and enlightened. I definetely recommend everyone watch it. I can give a brief introduction, but agian you earn much more if you watch it yourself and I bet you would enjoy it.

1\. Don't Multi-task, Be Present. Focus on that Conversation. Eg, don't think about what you will have for dinner, what your boss just told you.

2\. Don't Pontificate. Don't state your hard opinion unless it carries on the conversation. (I think). If you strongly disagree with someone or an opinion, write a blog.

3\. Enter every conservation assuming you have something to learn and use who what when where and why. Let the other one describe the situation. Be open. Instead asking "Are you sacred", asking "how do you feel"?

4\. Don't stop listening. Let thoughts ebb and flow continue focusing on the conservation. You might have a good idea during the conversation, but don't just thinking about the idea yop of your mind, keep focusing on the topic

5\. Admit to not knowing something. Air on the side of caution, don't pretend to be an expert. If you don't know something, just say it. No one is expected to be perfect.

6\. Don't assume your experiences are their experiences  All experiences are unique. Give more context and listen to them.

7\. Don't repeat yourself. If you need to say something more then twice, considering writing a blog. Similar to SDE work, if you need to use it three times, automate it.

8\. Details don't matter, leave them out. No need to be specific the time, the place, people care about you, your stories.

9. LISTEN. Resist the urge to talk, and avoid being distracted by random thoughts. From some perspective, talk is eaiser and listening is hard. We need to control ourselves to listen as more as we can.

10\. Be Brief
