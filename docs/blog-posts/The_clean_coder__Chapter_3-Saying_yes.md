There are three parts to making a commitment:

1.  1\. You say it.
    
2.  2\. You mean it.
    
3.  3\. You actually do it.
    

Instead of trusting our guts, we can use some language-related tricks to try and figure out if people really mean what they say. And that’s not okay when you or someone else relies on those promises as part of the job.

Don’t say it is out of my control. The real truth is that you, personally, always have something that’s under your control, so there is always something you can fully commit to doing. The secret ingredient to recognizing real commitment is to look for sentences that sounds like: I will…by… (I will finish this by Tuesday)

What’s important about this sentence? You are starting a fact about something YOU will do with a clear end time. You are not talking about someone else, you are talking about you. You are talking about “possibly” or “might get to it”, you will achieve it.

Raise red flag early if you cannot do it.

If you are not tell anyone about the problem as soon as possible, you are not giving anyone a chance to help you follow through on your commitment.

“I’ll call home and clear some overtime with my family. If they are ok with it. Then I’ll get this task done by Monday Morning. I’ll even come in on Monday morning to make sure everything goes smoothly with Willy. But then I’ll go home and won’t be back until Wednesday. Deal?” — This is perfectly fair.

Professionals are not required to say yes to everything that is asked of them. However, they would word hard to find creative ways to make “yes” possible. When professionals say yes, they will use the language of commitment so that there is no doubt about what’s they promised.

My comments:

This chapter talks about how do you say yes. You always need to give certainty to business or manager. In any case, there is something you can be certain about and you can say “I will finish something by sometime”. So you can earn more trust by doing that since when you say yes, you actually get it done; when you say no, it is impossible. Since managers manage a lot of projects and business needs a certain date, this give them much more visibility.
